Pairing and Feedback

1/24/15

Pairing can be hit and miss at times depending on who your partner is, if your personalities mesh, and your own state of mind. If I am frazzled, distracted, and all over the place, I will probably be difficult to pair with. Usually I like to take a break an hour or so before the pairing session to unwind a little. Then I'll spend the rest of the time looking over the releases for the challenge. Then the fun begins.

I've genuinely had fun in some pairing sessions. Usually we were both focused on the challenge whiched helped us solve it without relative difficulty. Since I'm more reserved I usually pair better with someone who is more comfortable taking the lead. But I know I will not always find such ideal circumstances, so I have been practicing leading more when it is evident that I have more knowledge on the subject than they do. The tricky part, I find, is when we are both equally knowledgable on the subject. Then I feel like I'm trying not to step on their toes.

Funnily enough, I found giving feedback more difficult than the actual pairing. Maybe because I was too wrapped up in what I was doing rather than focusing on what my partner was doing. So recalling specifics on what they did was kind of hard. Also, when I have a pair that didn't put forth enough effort or made a mistake, I still think they will be able to tell it's me giving the feedback, even though I know it's anonymous. Weird. So overall I think I need to be less self-concious and more externally focused.

As for receiving feedback, it has been okay because it's mostly been positive so far. The main critcique I get is to be more vocal, which I agree with. Talking through a problem is a little foreign to me, since I'm usually working through this stuff myself. To improve this I think I'll try talking to myself as I work on a problem, especially when reading through code.
