Learning & Thinking Style

1-11-2015

My score from the Gregoric style of thinking was a tie. A tie? Yup, 36 and 36. Well that's interesting. But wait it gets better. The categories that tied were Concrete Sequential and Abstract Random, two seemingly opposite styles of thinking. A CS thinker finds it difficult to work in groups, while the AR thinker thrives in group activities. The AR thinker finds it difficult to work in a restrictive environment while the CS thinker thrives on order, structure and predictability. The CS thinker has problems "dealing with abstract ideas." And the AR thinker has difficulty "giving exact details." Faced with this information I wondered if I might have a learning disability.

All joking aside, I do see parts of myself reflected in each group. I like order and logic, but I have problems concentrating on one thing at a time. When building my website I was constantly getting distracted by the newest problem and found it hard to stay focused on one task. And I do like to work with people, but only within the right environment. If the vibe is too cold, or formal, I'd rather work alone. Not that I hate formalities though, I often rely on them in social situations. But at the same time I find them boring and repetitive.

I think what this says about my learning style is that I behave differently in different environments. I think in an ideal social learning situation where I am comfortable I tend to be a more abstract random learner. But when uncomfortable I tend to be more concrete sequential. So my comfort level will be a good indicator for which learning strategies I employ. I could possibly see a shift as DBC progresses. It would be natural to feel a little uncomfortable during the first phase because everything is new; the environment, the people, the work. I will probably lean toward CS methods. But as the course moves on and I grow more comfortable with my peers and environment I will bounce back to more AR methods. Therefore I should be sure to give myself plenty of space and time to study alone while in phase 1 of the course. But not too much as I do want to get to know my classmates and instructors. The key will be how quickly I acclimate. I think pairing will definitely help me get to know people and I should try to pair with as many NYC boots as possible. Then once I gravitate to a more natural AR learning style I will focus on maintaining these relationships so I can work in an ideal environment.

The growth vs. fixed mindset debate focuses on whether or not intelligence and other attributes can be changed. I saw a lot of myself in the fixed mindset group. I tend to try to prove myself a lot, and will avoid challenges as they may be opportunities to fail. In order to become more growth oriented I can follow the four steps Dweck outlines in her book: learn to hear the fixed mindset "voice", recognize that I have a choice, assume a growth mindset attitude, and finally take the growth mindset action. If I do this I'm confident I can get out of the fixed mindset.
